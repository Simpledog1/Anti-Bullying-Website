Laws & Policies (Philippines)

Philippine law protects students from bullying and requires schools to take action.

Republic Act No. 10627 – Anti-Bullying Act of 2013

Main Points:

  • Requires all elementary and secondary schools to adopt policies to prevent and address bullying
  • Defines bullying and directs schools to prohibit bullying on or off campus
  • Establishes clear procedures for reporting and handling bullying incidents
  • Mandates training for teachers and school staff on bullying prevention
  • Requires schools to create a Child Protection Committee
  • Sets guidelines for investigating and resolving bullying cases

Who is Protected:

  • Students (victims) – Those who experience bullying in any form
  • Witnesses – Those who see bullying happen and come forward
  • Reporters – Those who report bullying incidents to authorities

The law ensures protection from retaliation for anyone who reports bullying.

Why This Law Matters:

Republic Act No. 10627 is important because it protects us students from bullying and helps make schools safer. It reminds us students to respect each other, avoid bullying, and be careful with our words and actions. This law also educates schools to take responsibility in handling bullying cases properly in any schools. Because of this, students can feel more confident to report problems without fear. Overall, the Anti-Bullying Act helps us create a calm and friendly learning environment.

A Criminology Student's Perspective:

"Republic Act No. 10627 emphasizes the responsibility of schools to protect students by clearly defining bullying and requiring policies to address it. As a 2nd year criminology student, I view this law as a preventive measure that helps stop harmful behavior early before it escalates into more serious offenses."

What Schools Must Do:

  • Post anti-bullying policies in visible areas within the school
  • Conduct regular information campaigns about bullying
  • Provide counseling services for both victims and bullies
  • Involve parents in addressing bullying incidents
  • Document all bullying cases and actions taken
  • Submit annual reports on bullying incidents to DepEd
