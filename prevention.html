Prevention & Support

How to Prevent & Stop Bullying

We all have a role to play in creating safe, respectful communities.

Speak Up

If You're Being Bullied: Tell a trusted adult immediately. You are not alone, and help is available. It's not your fault. Don't suffer in silence – reach out to a teacher, parent, school counselor, or call the hotlines listed on our contact page.

Stand Together

If You Witness Bullying: Don't stay silent. Report it to a teacher, parent, or school counselor. Your voice matters. Standing up for others can make all the difference. You can be the change that stops bullying.

For Students:

Help kids understand bullying and how to stand up to it safely. Keep the lines of communication open by checking in with kids often and listening to them. Encourage kids to do what they love so they can build confidence and make friends. Model how to treat others with kindness and respect.

Us students have an important role in avoiding bullying in school. We should always treat others with kindness, respect and in a friendly way, even if they are different from us. Not knowing the person doesn't mean you can bully that person. Instead of supporting negative behavior, we should stand up for what is right and protect those who are being bullied. Reporting bullying problems to teachers or school guidance can help stop the problem of bullying. When we support one another, we can create a calm, safe and friendly school environment.

Key Point: Bullying prevention starts with educating students about what bullying is and encouraging them to stand up safely for themselves and others. Open communication, positive role models, and supporting students' interests help build confidence and promote respect within the school community.

Creating Safe Environments

Safe Learning Spaces

Creating environments where students feel protected and supported in their education. Schools must provide physical and emotional safety for all students.

Positive Activities

Encouraging healthy social interactions through sports and recreational programs. Positive activities help build friendships and reduce isolation.

Community Support

Building strong connections between students, teachers, and families. A supportive community makes everyone feel valued and protected.
